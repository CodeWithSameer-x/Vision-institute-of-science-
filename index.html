Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

What You Will Learn

Understand the fundamental concepts of chemistry, including matter, its classification, laws of chemical combinations, and mole concept. Learn to solve numerical problems related to stoichiometry and concentration terms.

Structure of Atom

What You Will Learn

Explore atomic models, subatomic particles, and quantum numbers. Study Bohrâ€™s model, quantum mechanical model, and electronic configuration of elements.

Classification of Elements and Periodicity in Properties

What You Will Learn

Learn about the periodic table, periodic trends in properties like atomic radius, ionization energy, and electronegativity. Understand the significance of periodic classification.

Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure

What You Will Learn

Study types of chemical bonds, VSEPR theory, valence bond theory, and molecular orbital theory. Understand hybridization, bond parameters, and intermolecular forces.

States of Matter

What You Will Learn

Explore the properties of gases, liquids, and solids. Learn gas laws, kinetic theory of gases, and intermolecular interactions affecting states of matter.

Thermodynamics

What You Will Learn

Understand thermodynamic terms, laws of thermodynamics, and their applications in chemical processes. Study enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy.

Equilibrium

What You Will Learn

Learn about chemical and ionic equilibrium, Le Chatelierâ€™s principle, equilibrium constants, and acid-base concepts. Solve problems related to pH and buffer solutions.

Redox Reactions

What You Will Learn

Study oxidation and reduction processes, balancing redox reactions, and calculating oxidation numbers. Understand electrochemical cells and their applications.

Hydrogen

What You Will Learn

Explore the properties, preparation, and uses of hydrogen. Study hydrides, water, heavy water, and hydrogen peroxide.

s-Block Elements

What You Will Learn

Learn about the properties, preparation, and compounds of alkali and alkaline earth metals. Understand their biological significance and chemical reactivity.

p-Block Elements

What You Will Learn

Study the properties and compounds of p-block elements, focusing on groups 13 and 14. Explore their trends in physical and chemical properties.

Organic Chemistry: Some Basic Principles and Techniques

What You Will Learn

Understand the basics of organic chemistry, including nomenclature, isomerism, and purification techniques. Learn about reaction mechanisms and functional groups.

Hydrocarbons

What You Will Learn

Explore the classification, preparation, and reactions of alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, and aromatic hydrocarbons. Study their properties and applications.

Solutions

What You Will Learn

Study types of solutions, concentration terms, colligative properties, and Raoultâ€™s law. Learn to solve numerical problems related to solutions.

Electrochemistry

What You Will Learn

Understand electrochemical cells, Nernst equation, and conductance in electrolytic solutions. Study batteries, fuel cells, and corrosion.

Chemical Kinetics

What You Will Learn

Learn about the rate of reactions, factors affecting reaction rates, and rate laws. Study order, molecularity, and integrated rate equations.

The d & f Block Elements

What You Will Learn

Explore the properties, electronic configuration, and compounds of transition and inner transition elements. Study their applications and chemical behavior.

Coordination Compounds

What You Will Learn

Understand the structure, nomenclature, and bonding in coordination compounds. Study isomerism, valence bond theory, and crystal field theory.

Haloalkanes and Haloarenes

What You Will Learn

Learn about the preparation, properties, and reactions of haloalkanes and haloarenes. Study nucleophilic substitution and elimination reactions.

Alcohols, Phenols and Ethers

What You Will Learn

Study the nomenclature, preparation, and reactions of alcohols, phenols, and ethers. Understand their physical properties and chemical reactivity.

Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids

What You Will Learn

Explore the preparation, properties, and reactions of aldehydes, ketones, and carboxylic acids. Study nucleophilic addition and oxidation reactions.

Amines

What You Will Learn

Learn about the classification, preparation, and reactions of amines. Study their basicity, diazonium salts, and applications in organic synthesis.

Biomolecules

What You Will Learn

Understand the structure and functions of carbohydrates, proteins, nucleic acids, and lipids. Study enzymes and their role in biological processes.

Reproduction in Lower and Higher Plants

What You Will Learn

Explore the reproductive processes in plants, including asexual and sexual reproduction, pollination, fertilization, and seed formation. Understand the differences between lower and higher plants.

Reproduction in Lower and Higher Animals

What You Will Learn

Learn about reproductive strategies in animals, from simple organisms to complex vertebrates. Study asexual and sexual reproduction, reproductive organs, and embryonic development.

Inheritance and Variation

What You Will Learn

Understand the principles of genetics, Mendel's laws, patterns of inheritance, and causes of genetic variation. Explore concepts like dominance, co-dominance, and linkage.

Molecular Basis of Inheritance

What You Will Learn

Dive into the molecular mechanisms of heredity, including DNA structure, replication, transcription, translation, and gene regulation. Study the role of RNA and genetic code.

Origin and Evolution of Life

What You Will Learn

Explore the theories of the origin of life and the processes of evolution. Learn about natural selection, speciation, and evidence of evolution through fossils and molecular biology.

Plant Water Relation

What You Will Learn

Study how plants absorb, transport, and utilize water. Understand osmosis, transpiration, and the role of xylem in water movement.

Plant Growth and Mineral Nutrition

What You Will Learn

Learn about plant growth phases, hormones, and the role of essential minerals in plant nutrition. Explore deficiency symptoms and nutrient uptake mechanisms.

Respiration and Circulation

What You Will Learn

Understand the processes of respiration and circulation in plants and animals. Study cellular respiration, glycolysis, and the circulatory system in humans.

Control and Coordination

What You Will Learn

Explore how organisms coordinate activities through nervous and endocrine systems. Study neurons, synapses, and hormonal regulation in humans.

Human Health and Diseases

What You Will Learn

Learn about common diseases, their causes, prevention, and treatment. Study the immune system, infectious diseases, and lifestyle-related disorders.

Enhancement of Food Production

What You Will Learn

Discover techniques to improve food production, including plant breeding, tissue culture, and animal husbandry. Explore sustainable agricultural practices.

Biotechnology

What You Will Learn

Understand the principles of biotechnology, including genetic engineering, recombinant DNA technology, and their applications in medicine and agriculture.

Organisms and Populations

What You Will Learn

Study the interactions between organisms and their environments. Learn about population dynamics, adaptations, and ecological relationships.

Ecosystems and Energy Flow

What You Will Learn

Explore the structure and function of ecosystems. Understand energy flow, food chains, food webs, and nutrient cycles.

Biodiversity, Conservation and Environmental Issues

What You Will Learn

Learn about biodiversity, its importance, threats, and conservation strategies. Study environmental issues like pollution, climate change, and sustainable development.

About Vision Institute of Science

We offer specialized coaching for students of Class 9 & 10 (Science Foundation) and Class 11 & 12 (Biology & Chemistry), along with targeted preparation for competitive exams like NEET.

  • Support for NEET preparation with extra Chemistry classes.
